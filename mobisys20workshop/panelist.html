Panelist Biography

Santosh Kumar (University of Memphis)
Biography: Santosh Kumar is the Lillian & Morrie Moss Chair of Excellence Professor of Computer Science at The University of Memphis. He is also Director of NIH Center of Excellence for Mobile Sensor Data-to-Knowledge (MD2K). His research interests include machine learning (ML) models for inference of human health and daily behaviors from wearable sensors, open-source mHealth software platforms, and behavioral privacy. His research has resulted in ML models for detecting stress, speaking, smoking, craving, cocaine use, brushing, and flossing from physiological and inertial sensors. Open-source software developed by MD2K include mCerebrum for smartphones and Cerebral Cortex for the cloud that have been adopted in scientific studies by clinical and behavioral science researchers in 11 states across USA.

Majid Sarrafzadeh (University of California, Los Angeles)
Biography: Prof. Sarrafzadeh is currently a Distinguished Professor of Computer Science and Electrical Engineering. He is a co-founder and co-director of the Center for SMART Health. His recent research interests lie in the area of Embedded Computing and Data Analytics with emphasis on healthcare. Dr. Sarrafzadeh is a Fellow of IEEE. Professor Sarrafzadeh has published more than 550 papers, co-authored 5 books, and is a named inventor on many US patents. Dr. Sarrafzadeh has collaborated with many industries in the past 30 years. Cecilia Mascolo (University of Cambridge)
Biography: Cecilia Mascolo is the mother of a teenage daughter but also a full professor of Mobile Systems in the Department of Computer Science and Technology at the University of Cambridge. She is co-director of the Centre for Mobile, Wearable System and Augmented Intelligence and the recipient of an ERC Advanced Research Grant. Prior joining Cambridge in 2008, she has been a faculty member in the Department of Computer Science at University College London. She holds a PhD from the University of Bologna. Her research interests are in mobile systems for health, human mobility modeling, sensor systems and networking and mobile data analysis. She has published in a number of top tier conferences and journals in the area and her investigator experience spans projects funded by Research Councils and industry. She has received numerous best paper awards and has served as steering, organizing and programme committee member of mobile, sensor systems, networking, data science conferences and workshops. She has delivered a number of keynote talks at conferences and workshops in the area of mobility, data science, pervasive computing and systems. More details can be found at http://www.cl.cam.ac.uk/users/cm542.

Wendy Nielsen (National Science Foundation)
Biography: Wendy Nilsen, Ph.D. is a Program Director for the Smart and Connected Health Program in the Directorate for Computer & Information Science & Engineering at the National Science Foundation. Her work focuses on the intersection of technology and health. This includes a wide range of methods for data collection, data analytics and turning data to knowledge. Her efforts include: serving as cochair of the Health Information Technology Research and Development community of practice of the Networking and Information Technology Research and Development Program; the lead for the NSF/NIH Smart and Connected Health announcement; convening workshops to address methodology in mobile technology research; serving on numerous federal technology initiatives; and, leading training institutes. Previously, Wendy was at the NIH Office of Behavioral and Social Sciences Research (OBSSR).
